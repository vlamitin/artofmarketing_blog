📍 For almost 5 years I worked in a digital agency, and I looked at clients' issues as a contractor. After that, I spent three more years working on the client side, and I noticed a huge difference in the approach. My findings and insides from the business are in the article below. Everything described here is purely my work experience in large corporations. And they could be considered as a list of recommendations for agencies. 
![](connection.jpg)
1️⃣ **Business mood of contractors is crucial for partnership between agencies & clients**. Contractors from agencies are often psychologically unstable, and they got quickly deflated, especially when working with large advertisers. Only a few out of hundreds really do understand the essence of customer service (not taking things personally, not putting the client in stupid situations, knowing that "I do not know" is not the answer, making only realistic promises, etc.). Bottom line is that only few people know how to love a customer in a healthy way. The client will always be grateful for the reasonable autonomy and initiative by the contractor, for the simplification of processes in favor of speed and optimization. Because a good contractor is the merit of the client who works with him (it is the client who found good partners, it is the client who set up a process of interaction). Such agency helps to fulfill the plan, and allows the client to have full control. The contractor is the engine, not the cargo.

2️⃣ **Agencies should understand well the clients' business goals**. The big client always has the general sales plan (top tier) for the year/quarter/month, where digital ads is considered as one of many tools, and it has its share (below 100%). As a agency, you need to keep this in mind in your forecasts and plans, and you should have arguments why you won't reach or why you can reach a bigger share in the overall plan. The plan moves from top to bottom, and the adapted forecast moves from bottom to top.

3️⃣ **Help the client to defend your ideas inside his company**. When working on the client side, you always need to answer the questions like: "what will this advertising give us?", "will it be brand recognition or direct sales?", "how much will we spend?", "how much will we get?". So any digital plan will be defended equally with the offline media plan and equally with other plans. Each plan's utility ratio will be compared to each other in terms of cost/profit now/profit later. Therefore, it will not be redundant to choose the right arguments that will be clear to people who are not from digital.

4️⃣ **Help corporations to go online**. A lot of top managers know a lot about offline advertising and don't know how work with digital ads. There is a correlation with the age: those top managers who give money for advertising are usually 40+ years old. They usually do understand offline ads, but digital advertising is a black box for them. You have to be ready to explain complicated things with simple words, to translate arguments from the language of digital to the language of offline.

5️⃣ **Find out how to count unique users**. A lot of clients want to count unique users covered, and ideally they want to understand how much they've paid for the ad being shown to a real user (not a cookie). It's super important, but unrealizable in current realities. Not everyone understands that it's impossible to properly calculate (it's digital, damn!) those things, so we keep counting it approximately. Ideally, one should learn how to manage the distribution of ads between unique users, manage the price per ad being shown to the real person.

6️⃣ **Adequate people on the client side are your friends**. People who fully understand at least one small process and can manage it adequately are almost don't exist. In fast-growing companies the processes are often chaotic, and you get a mess of everything. A person who can make something turnkey (prepare, run, summarize the results, draw conclusions) is worth their weight in gold. Therefore, plan that processes on the client's side will not run smoothly.

7️⃣ **Don't forget loyalty and retention users**. It is important for businesses not only to attract customers, but also to retain them and to develop their check. For that purpose digital agencies often do not have enough energy or time, or it has low priority right now. But in the end, the greatest profit for the business comes from loyal clients, and that's why their total share should be constantly increased. The winner is the agency that looks ahead and offers the solution to retain users at the same as attract new ones. Don't forget to calculate it in your ad offers (but remember: it depends on clients' business). 

Not all the businesses have these details, but those who work with digital advertising definitely have them. Use this information individually for your situation and pass it through a sieve of common sense.
